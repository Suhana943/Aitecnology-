Probability Theory and Basic Rules

Probability theory quantifies uncertainty, serving as the backbone for machine learning algorithms that predict outcomes based on data.

1. Fundamental Rules

  • Sum Rule: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) for disjoint events.
  • Product Rule: P(A and B) = P(A) * P(B) for independent events.
  • Complement Rule: P(not A) = 1 - P(A).

2. Coding Probability

import random
# Simulate 10,000 coin flips
flips = [random.choice(['Heads', 'Tails']) for _ in range(10000)]
prob_heads = flips.count('Heads') / 10000
print(f"Prob: {prob_heads:.4f}")
Aitecnology Tip: In AI, we often deal with Conditional Probability (P(A|B)). Always remember: P(A|B) is NOT the same as P(B|A)!
