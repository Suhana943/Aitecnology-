Central Limit Theorem for Data Science

By: Aitecnology Team | Published: July 9, 2026

The Central Limit Theorem (CLT) is the statistical phenomenon where the distribution of sample means approaches a normal distribution as sample size increases, regardless of the original population distribution.

1. Key Significance

  • Universality: Bridges non-normal data with normal distribution statistical methods.
  • Inference: Enables confidence intervals and hypothesis testing on diverse datasets.
  • Efficiency: Allows us to estimate population parameters from manageable sample sizes.

2. Implementation Example

import numpy as np

# Sample means from a Uniform distribution
population = np.random.uniform(0, 1, 10000)
sample_means = [np.mean(np.random.choice(population, size=50)) for _ in range(1000)]

print(f"Mean: {np.mean(sample_means):.4f}")
Aitecnology Tip: The CLT typically "kicks in" once your sample size n is at least 30. If your data is heavily skewed or contains extreme outliers, you may need a larger sample size to achieve a stable normal distribution.
