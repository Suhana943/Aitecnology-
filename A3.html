Mastering Control Flow and Loops

Published: July 10, 2026

What is a Loop?

Imagine you need to say "Hello" to 100 people. Instead of writing 100 lines of code, you use a "Loop." A loop tells your program to perform the same task repeatedly until a specific goal is reached.

[attachment_0](attachment)

Types of Loops

1. For Loop (The "Counted" Loop)

Use this when you know exactly how many times the action should repeat. It is perfect for iterating through a list of data.

# Example: Printing a sequence of numbers
for i in range(1, 6):
    print("Number:", i)

2. While Loop (The "Conditional" Loop)

This keeps running as long as a specific condition is True. It stops only when the condition becomes False.

# Example: Charging a phone until 100%
battery = 0
while battery < 100:
    print(f"Phone is charging... {battery}%")
    battery += 20
print("Charging complete!")

Why are Loops important in AI?

  • Saves Time: Processes massive datasets in seconds.
  • Data Analysis: Easily check millions of rows for specific values.
  • Model Training: Loops are used to run "epochs" (training cycles) in machine learning to help the model learn.
  • Automation: Performs repetitive tasks like resizing thousands of images automatically.
Aitecnology Tip: Always include an exit condition in your while loops to avoid an "Infinite Loop," which can freeze your computer!
