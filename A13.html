Dot Products and Inner Products Explained

By: Aitecnology Team | Published: July 9, 2026

In Linear Algebra, the Dot Product and Inner Product are fundamental ways to measure the relationship between two vectors. In AI, these are the primary math operations used to determine "similarity" between data points.

1. What is a Dot Product?

The dot product takes two vectors and returns a single number (a scalar). This number tells you how much the two vectors "align" with each other.

2. Why use them in AI?

  • Measuring Similarity: Used in cosine similarity to compare how close two data items are.
  • Neural Networks: The core mechanism of weight and input multiplication in deep learning layers.

3. Coding Example

import numpy as np
vector_a = np.array([1, 2, 3])
vector_b = np.array([4, 5, 6])
result = np.dot(vector_a, vector_b) # Returns 32
print(result)
Aitecnology Tip: If your dot product result is 0, the vectors are Orthogonal (at a 90-degree angle), meaning they are totally unrelated in your dataset.
