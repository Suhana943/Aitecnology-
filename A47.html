Unsupervised Learning: DBSCAN Density Clustering

Published: July 9, 2026 | Category: ML

DBSCAN (Density-Based Spatial Clustering of Applications with Noise) is a powerful clustering algorithm that, unlike K-Means, does not require you to specify the number of clusters in advance. Instead, it groups together points that are closely packed, effectively identifying clusters of arbitrary shapes and separating them from noise.

How DBSCAN Works

DBSCAN relies on two parameters: eps (maximum distance for neighborhood) and min_samples (density threshold).

  • Core Points: Points with at least min_samples neighbors within eps distance.
  • Border Points: Points within eps of a core point but with fewer neighbors.
  • Noise: Points that don't belong to any cluster.

Why Use DBSCAN?

  • Arbitrary Shapes: Discovers clusters of any shape, unlike K-Means.
  • No K Needed: The number of clusters is discovered automatically.
  • Robustness: Naturally identifies and ignores noise.

Quick Example

from sklearn.cluster import DBSCAN
from sklearn.datasets import make_moons

# Generate non-spherical data
X, _ = make_moons(n_samples=200, noise=0.05)

# Initialize and fit DBSCAN
# eps: neighborhood radius, min_samples: density threshold
dbscan = DBSCAN(eps=0.2, min_samples=5)
labels = dbscan.fit_predict(X)

print("Unique clusters found:", set(labels))
