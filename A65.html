RNNs: Foundations of Sequence Models

Published: July 9, 2026 | Category: Deep Learning

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) are designed to handle sequential data by maintaining a hidden state that carries information across time steps.

Core Concepts

  • Hidden State: Acts as the network's internal memory.
  • Weight Sharing: The same parameters are applied to each step in the sequence.
  • Vanishing Gradient: A major challenge where gradients fade during long sequences, limiting memory capacity.

Quick Example

# Simple RNN in PyTorch
rnn = nn.RNN(input_size=10, hidden_size=20, batch_first=True)
output, hn = rnn(input_seq, h0)
