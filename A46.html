Unsupervised Learning: K-Means Clustering

Published: July 9, 2026 | Category: ML

K-Means is one of the most fundamental and widely used unsupervised learning algorithms. Its goal is to partition a dataset into K distinct, non-overlapping groups (clusters), where each data point belongs to the cluster with the nearest mean (centroid).

How K-Means Works

The algorithm operates through a simple iterative process:

  • Initialization: Choose K and randomly place K centroids.
  • Assignment: Assign each data point to the nearest centroid based on Euclidean distance.
  • Update: Recalculate the position of each centroid by taking the mean of its assigned data points.
  • Repeat: Continue until the centroids no longer move significantly.

Choosing the Right K

We use the Elbow Method to choose K: plot the "Within-Cluster Sum of Squares" (WCSS) against different values of K and look for the point where the rate of decrease shifts sharply.

Quick Example

from sklearn.cluster import KMeans
from sklearn.datasets import make_blobs

# Generate sample data
X, _ = make_blobs(n_samples=300, centers=4)

# Apply K-Means
kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=4, init='k-means++')
kmeans.fit(X)

# Get cluster assignments
labels = kmeans.labels_
print("Cluster labels for the first 10 points:", labels[:10])
