Regularization Techniques: Dropout Layers

Published: July 9, 2026 | Category: Deep Learning

Dropout is a powerful regularization technique used to combat overfitting. By randomly deactivating neurons during training, it prevents complex co-adaptations and encourages the network to learn more robust, distributed features.

How It Works

  • Random Deactivation: During each training step, neurons are dropped based on probability p.
  • Ensemble Effect: It creates a form of bagging that approximates training multiple sub-networks.
  • Inference Phase: Dropout is automatically disabled during evaluation for full performance.

Quick Example

# Applying dropout in a PyTorch layer
self.dropout = nn.Dropout(p=0.5) 

# Used in the forward pass
x = self.dropout(x)
