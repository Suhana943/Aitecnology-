Calculus: Understanding Derivatives

By: Aitecnology Team | Published: July 9, 2026

Calculus is the study of continuous change. The derivative is a mathematical tool that measures exactly how fast a function changes at any given moment. In machine learning, derivatives are the backbone of optimization, enabling models to "learn" by minimizing errors.

1. What is a Derivative?

A derivative represents the instantaneous rate of change of a function with respect to one of its variables. Geometrically, it is the slope of the tangent line to the curve at a specific point. If a function is f(x), its derivative is denoted as f'(x) or df/dx.

2. Why are they Important?

  • Optimization: Helps find the minimum or maximum values (e.g., minimizing model error).
  • Gradient Descent: The primary algorithm for training neural networks.
  • Sensitivity Analysis: Measures how an output shifts with small changes in input.

3. How Derivatives Work

Derivatives tell us the slope: a positive slope indicates the function is increasing, a negative slope indicates it is decreasing, and a slope of zero indicates a peak or valley (extrema).

4. Coding Example (Automatic Differentiation)

We use libraries like PyTorch to calculate derivatives automatically:

import torch

# Define x with gradient tracking
x = torch.tensor(3.0, requires_grad=True)

# Define function f(x) = x^2
y = x**2

# Calculate derivative
y.backward()

# Result of 2x at x=3 is 6
print(f"The derivative at x=3 is: {x.grad.item()}")

5. Key Concepts

  • Chain Rule: Used to derive composite functions; critical for backpropagation.
  • Partial Derivatives: Measures change in one variable while keeping others constant.
Aitecnology Tip: Remember that gradients point in the direction of steepest ascent. To train your model, you move in the opposite direction—this is why it is called Gradient Descent!
