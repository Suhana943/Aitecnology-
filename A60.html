Regularization Techniques: Batch Normalization

Published: July 9, 2026 | Category: Deep Learning

Batch Normalization (BatchNorm) stabilizes training by normalizing the activations of each layer, ensuring they maintain a stable mean and variance. This allows for faster convergence and deeper network architectures.

How It Works

  • Normalization: Centers inputs to zero mean and unit variance.
  • Scale and Shift: Learnable parameters (gamma and beta) allow the network to recover the original activation distribution if needed.
  • Stabilization: Mitigates internal covariate shift, allowing for higher learning rates.

Quick Example

# Applying batch norm in a PyTorch layer
self.bn1 = nn.BatchNorm1d(64)

# Used in the forward pass
x = self.bn1(x)
